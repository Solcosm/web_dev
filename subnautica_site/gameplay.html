GAMEPLAY

Learn About the Kind of Gameplay Subnautica Offers

The player controls the protagonist (dubbed the Lone Survivor) from a first-person perspective. The main objective of the game is to explore the different biomes of Planet 4546b while tending to their health, hunger, thirst, and oxygen meters. Players can interact with the environment by gathering resources such as scrap metal and the local flora and fauna. Using these resources, the player can construct food, tools, submersible vehicles, and structures which they can use to create personalized bases. The game also features an active day/night cycle which affects the hostility of various sea creatures.

Various game modes are included in the most recent build of the game (April 2017) which include:

  • Survival
    • The default mode for Subnautica in which the player must maintain their oxygen, health, hunger, and thirst meters to stay alive.
    • Death causes the player to lose all the items in their inventory unless they sync up with their base.
  • Freedom
    • Like survival with the exception being that the player does not need to maintain a thirst or hunger meter.
    • Oxygen and health still apply to player deaths.
    • Death causes the player to lose all the items in their inventory unless they sync up with their base.
  • Hardcore
    • Like survival with the exception being that the player is restricted to a single life.
    • When the player dies the game ends and the player is forced to start from the very beginning.
    • The in-game navigator also does not warn the player when their oxygen supply is running low.
  • Creative
    • Creative mode offers the player with complete freedom.
    • Removal of the hunger, thirst, health, and oxygen meters allow for the player to travel anywhere on the map without fear of death.
    • The player also begins the game with every unlockable item and has unlimited resources for construction.
    • The story is also disabled for immersion and balance purposes.
