“War never changes.”

Fallout is a series of post-nuclear apocalypse role-playing video games. Set sometime during the 22nd and 23rd centuries, the theme of the Fallout games relies heavily on the post-cold war culture of 1950s America. Combining the imaginary and futuristic technology along with the ever-present looming fear of nuclear annihilation from an all-out war with China, the setting is a perfect blend of past and future.

In an alternate reality where the transistor was not invented and vacuum tubes and atomic physics take its place as the cornerstones of scientific progress, the world of Fallout is destined to be set in a 1950s era culture far into the future. This divergence allows for many bizarre technological occurrences such as advanced robotics, nuclear-powered automobiles and energy weapons being placed along room sized computers and rotary telephones.

After the self-proclaimed “golden age”, the United States begins to divide into 13 commonwealths which become controlled by a group of political conglomerates known as the Enclave. After the Enclave gain control over most of the United States, an energy crisis emerged due to depleting petroleum reserves (thanks to all the new technology) which leads to the “Resource Wars”. The Resource Wars feature smaller skirmishes which all snowball into the “Great War of 2077” which consists of a two-hour nuclear barrage between many major nuclear powers. This war devastates the United States and most likely the rest of the world, which gives us the settings that we explore in the games.

Knowing that a global scale war was imminent, the United States government began working on a project in 2054 to construct “Vaults”. There would need to have been around 400,000 vaults constructed to comfortably accommodate the population of the United States, although only 122 were constructed before the war. Although these vaults were advertised as protection from the bombs and the resulting radiation, they were secretly designed to host (mainly unethical) experiments on their dwellers. In three out of the five main titles in the series the player begins by exiting a vault which thrusts them into the post-apocalyptic United States wasteland…

Fallout

Fallout is the first game in the series and therefore is the oldest. Released in 1997, this isometric RPG is known as the spiritual successor to Wasteland, the previous post-apocalyptic game that Interplay Productions/Entertainment worked on. The game takes many elements from its predecessor and improves upon them, mainly through the introduction of full sprites and textured graphics. One of the biggest improvements that was included was the extremely open-ended gameplay and clever writing. The protagonist (Vault Dweller) is tasked with retrieving a water chip in the Wasteland which will replace the broken one currently in their vault. Once this chip is retrieved the player is caught up in thwarting the plans of a group of mutants led by their leader, aptly known as Master.

Fallout 2

Released a year later in 1998, Fallout 2 is the sequel to Fallout and boasted many improvements over the original, including an improved engine, many Quality of Life tweaks to the companion characters and some combat changes while keeping its isometric perspective. Along with these changes the writing and game world were adjusted to include more pop culture jokes and self-parodying references. Eighty years after the events of the first game, the protagonist of Fallout 2 (a descendent of the protagonist of the previous game), known as the Chosen One, must save their village from severe droughts and famine. Afterwards the Chosen One must once again save the village from an assault by the Enclave.

Fallout 3

Spearheaded by Todd Howard and developed by Bethesda Game Studios, known for their work on The Elder Scrolls, the series saw a revival after a decade in the form of Fallout 3 which released in 2008. Rather than an isometric perspective with turn-based combat, Fallout 3 brought the series into the world of First-Person Shooters with real-time combat. This shift into a 3D made the open-world style of the previous games along with the world building talent of Bethesda turn the game into an instant classic. The protagonist, known as the Lone Wanderer, is forced to leave their home of Vault 101 after their father mysteriously departs. Once they emerge into the Capital Wasteland, the post-apocalyptic remnants of Washington D.C., the player sets off to find their father and discover the mysteries that the bombs created.

Fallout: New Vegas

Seen as a spinoff to Fallout 3 rather than a direct sequel, Fallout: New Vegas was developed by Obsidian Entertainment which included developers from the first two games. Released in 2010, New Vegas uses many of the same assets which were present in Fallout 3 and includes many new areas, quests, characters, weapons and a new overworld setting of the post-apocalyptic Mojave Desert. Due to Obsidian’s lengthy and detailed attention to world building and the lore of the series, New Vegas offers the player very detailed, interconnected and rich lore to discover. Having been aided by some developers of the original duology the writing in New Vegas features darker humor and tackles more taboo topics. The game is set four years after the events in the Capital Wasteland with the protagonist assuming the role of a courier who ends up getting shot in the head and left to die in a graveyard before being rescued by the doctor of Goodsprings. Once recovered, the courier sets off to find their would-be killer.

Fallout 4

Influenced by both Fallout 3 and Fallout: New Vegas the team at Bethesda Game Studios released a direct sequel to Fallout 3, aptly titled Fallout 4 in late 2015. Due to their previous work on The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim and their shift from the Gamebryo engine (used in Fallout 3 and Fallout: New Vegas) to their very own Creation Engine, Fallout 4 features many technical and graphical improvements over the past games. One of the biggest changes was the focus on improving the first-person combat and the introduction of a base building mechanic known as ‘Settlements’ where the player can create settlements for survivors in the wasteland. Set a decade after the events of Fallout 3 in 2287, the story begins on the day the Great War began: October 23, 2077. The protagonist, later to be known as the Sole Survivor, takes shelter in Vault 111 where they witness the murder of their spouse and the kidnapping of their son, Shaun. The Sole Survivor emerges 210 years later after being subject to suspended animation to roam the Boston Commonwealth in search of their missing son and to find out who murdered their spouse.
