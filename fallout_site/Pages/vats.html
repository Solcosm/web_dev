V.A.T.S.

“Sometimes survival requires a little gambling...”

V.A.T.S. is a core gameplay element that appears in every major installment since Fallout 3. Inspired by a similar ability to target an enemies specific body part in Fallout and Fallout 2, V.A.T.S. acts in a similar fashion for the 3D games – allowing the player to freeze/slow down time and target specific areas of the body. V.A.T.S. requires the usage of Action Points (AP) depending on the type of weapon being used and the area being targeted. When in the V.A.T.S. menu the player will see percentages, which indicate their likeliness to inflict a successful hit, the percentage chance caps out at 95% with a permanent 5% chance to miss. Small health meters are displayed over each targetable area which display the overall condition of that area with an empty bar meaning the limb is crippled. The bar at the top of the screen displays the targets overall health which is useful in determining which shots are worth taking.

There are many V.A.T.S. related perks and skills that the player can choose from depending on the game. Being first introduced to the player on their 10 th birthday in Vault 101 ( Fallout 3), V.A.T.S. becomes an integral part of the 3D games with many expansion perks including:

  • Action Boy/Girl
    • “Each level of Action Boy/Girl gives you an additional AP to spend every combat turn. You can use these generic APs on any task.” – Fallout 2 in-game description
  • Commando
    • “While using a rifle (or similar two-handed weapon), your accuracy in V.A.T.S. is significantly increased.” - In-game description, Fallout 3 and Fallout: New Vegas
  • Concentrated Fire
    • “With Concentrated Fire, your accuracy to hit any body part in V.A.T.S. increases slightly with each subsequent hit on that body part.” - In-game description
  • Grim Reaper’s Sprint
    • “If you kill a target while in V.A.T.S., all of your Action Points are restored upon exiting V.A.T.S.” - In-game description
  • Gunslinger
    • “While using a pistol (or similar one-handed weapon), your accuracy in V.A.T.S. is significantly increased.” - Fallout 3 in-game description
  • Mysterious Stranger
    • “Looks like you could use some assistance.” - Mysterious Stranger, Fallout
  • Nerves of Steel
    • “With the Nerves of Steel perk, you regenerate Action Points much more quickly than you normally would.” - In-game description
  • Paralyzing Palm
    • “With Paralyzing Palm, you will sometimes perform a special V.A.T.S. palm strike that paralyzes your opponent for 30 seconds. Note that in order to perform the Paralyzing Palm, you must be completely unarmed.” – Fallout 3 description
  • Sniper
    • “With the Sniper perk, your chance to hit an opponent's head in V.A.T.S. is significantly increased.” – Fallout 3 description
  • Wired Reflexes
    • “Advanced technology from the Commonwealth has increased your reaction speed, giving you a higher chance to hit in V.A.T.S.” – Fallout 3 description

“Created specifically to help our Wastelander relatives, the Vault-Tec Assisted Targeting System (V.A.T.S. for short) is designed to allow you to always have a chance to hit your targets! It works by manipulating space-time to allow our users to freeze/temporarily slow time to focus on specific body parts as targets.” – Vault-Tec Handbook
