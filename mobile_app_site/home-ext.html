Training and Education

Source

Developers must hold at minimum a Bachelor's degree (preferably in Computer Science or Information Technology) and must have a strong understanding of the programming languages that mobile devices use. Many degrees in this field involve concentrations in areas such as data structuring, systems design and general programming. Mastering multiple programming languages is also critical for software development.

Many software development positions require applicants to have some form of work experience, usually in the form in internships. These internships allow for novice developers to experience real-world deadlines and programming techniques. They also help aid in exposing developers to multi-team development projects and the process of releasing software "into the wild ".



Developer Certifications

Many positions will require a developer to have some kind of certification proving that they are fluent in a certain language or with a certain operating system.

There are many certification for the largest OSs and languages. For example, Google has an official Android Developer Certification that you can aquire by passing an exam.

Google Developers Certification lets you demonstrate your proficiency and skill. After you pass a certification exam, you can use your certification to promote yourself to the communities, projects, and employers that are important to you. All the certification exams are backed by rich training content offered as online curriculum, instructor-led training, or self-study materials. When you pass a certification exam, Google stores your certificate, badge, or other digital mark in a central, verifiable location. You can include your digital mark in your email signature and embed it on sites such as LinkedIn and Twitter.




Salary and Benefits

-Source [2] Source [1]

Mobile Software Development is a relatively new field, but the rise in popularity of mobile devices and the increasing ease of access to the internet has created an entirely new subset of software development jobs.

Introductory Mobile Application Developers tend to have a median annual salary of approximately $60,000[1] with senior level positions earning approximately $98,000 - $140,000[2].

Many firms provide benefits to software developers such as:

  • Access to the latest technology for testing and debugging code
  • Health, Dental and Vision Insurance
  • Generous vacation hours
  • Comfortable and easy to work in office environments
  • Diverse and fun team members
